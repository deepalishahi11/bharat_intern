Celsius (°C)

Celsius is a unit of temperature measurement. It is widely used in most of the world, especially in scientific contexts. Water boils at 100°C under standard atmospheric conditions.

Fahrenheit (°F)

Fahrenheit is a temperature scale used primarily in the United States. Water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F under standard atmospheric conditions.

Kelvin (K)

Kelvin is an absolute temperature scale used in scientific and engineering applications. It starts at absolute zero, where nothing could be colder.

Rankine (°R)

Rankine is a temperature scale that is rarely used but still has some applications, especially in engineering. It's similar to the Fahrenheit scale but starts at absolute zero.
